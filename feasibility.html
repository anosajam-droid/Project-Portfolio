The Challenge

A £40M commitment needs more than optimism

The College Project — 12 new facilities including student housing, a sports pavilion, and an assembly hall — was funded by a £40M bond at 4.5% interest over 45 years. The client needed to know, before committing to design, whether the project was financially viable across realistic, optimistic, and stress-tested scenarios.

This is RIBA Stage 1 work at its most consequential. Get the numbers wrong here and the project fails before it starts. Get them right and you give the client confidence to proceed.

The feasibility model isn't just a spreadsheet. It's the document that either unlocks a project or stops it — and it has to be honest about both outcomes.

What I Built

A model that runs for 45 years

I built a monthly developer's cash flow model running from January 2024 to 2069 — spanning pre-construction bond investment, 35 months of construction drawdown at £1.71M/month, phased rental income across 7 income streams, ongoing maintenance, and final bond repayment.

Every assumption was explicit and evidenced: construction costs from BCIS indices, rental rates from Rightmove, CIL rates from Oxford City Council, inflation modelled separately for tender and construction periods. The model discounted at 5% and returned a net present value of £8.5M — confirming viability across all scenarios.

Alongside the financial model I produced a full ISO 31000-aligned risk register — 17 risks scored by likelihood and impact, two at maximum severity, each with a named owner and mitigation strategy.
