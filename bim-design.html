The Approach

One model. Three disciplines. End-to-end.

The project started with a Revit 2024 model of a domestic dwelling — designed to UK Part L building regulations, with ground floor, first floor, full schedule of materials, and structural foundations. From that single model, three separate workstreams were run in parallel.

The first extracted quantity, cost and embodied carbon data directly from Revit schedules — no manual measurement. The second linked model elements to a construction programme in Navisworks for 4D simulation. The third federated two IFC models for clash detection between the MEP and structural disciplines.

Why It Matters

The gap between BIM awareness and BIM capability

Most people in construction have heard of BIM. Far fewer have actually extracted data from a model, run a 4D simulation, or connected model outputs to a business intelligence tool. This project demonstrates the difference.

The Power BI pipeline — Revit schedules exported to CSV, cleaned in Excel, imported to Power BI — is the same workflow used on live projects to generate real-time cost reports and carbon dashboards from federated models. Understanding this pipeline end-to-end is what separates a digitally capable PM from one who just knows the terminology.
