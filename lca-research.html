The Problem

A policy gap hiding in plain sight

The UK's net zero target depends on decarbonising construction. Life Cycle Assessment is the internationally recognised methodology for doing that — measuring environmental impact across a building's entire lifespan, from materials to demolition.

But LCA adoption is almost entirely confined to large contractors, publicly-funded schemes, and BREEAM-certified projects. SMEs — 99% of the industry, employing 60% of the workforce — have been systematically excluded from both the tools and the policy frameworks designed to drive decarbonisation.

Existing research documented the problem. Nobody had produced empirical evidence on what regulatory interventions might actually work for smaller firms operating on 3% margins.

Without mandates, SMEs will always see LCA as optional.

Industry practitioner — Senior engineer, facade systems & governance
My Approach

Evidence first, recommendations second

I designed a mixed-methods study combining three data sources — a sector-wide survey of 34 construction professionals, in-depth interviews with three practitioners at different career stages, and a comparative analysis of regulatory frameworks across the UK, Denmark, Norway, and Tampere (Finland).

The three sources were triangulated against each other: where survey data, interview discourse, and policy evidence converged, findings were treated as high-confidence. Where they diverged, I interrogated why — surfacing assumptions not visible from any single method alone.

The result is a set of recommendations grounded in what practitioners actually said, not what policy documents assumed. They treat SMEs as active contributors to decarbonisation — not just recipients of top-down mandates.

What the Evidence Showed

Four barriers. One system.

These aren't independent failures — they reinforce each other, making voluntary frameworks structurally inadequate.

79%
Cost is the dominant barrier
Software licensing, consultant fees, and staff time are unabsorbable on thin margins. The deeper problem: SMEs perceive LCA as a cost centre, not a strategic investment. No ROI pathway = no adoption.
59%
No mandate means no action
Of non-adopters, 59% cited absence of legal requirements as their primary reason. The UK's voluntary BEIS Action Plan has had negligible impact — confirmed across all three data sources independently.
71%
Client demand is missing
71% of respondents said clients prioritise short-term cost over sustainability — eliminating the market signal that would otherwise drive adoption without regulation. Procurement reform is non-negotiable.
35→59%
Mandates work — paired with support
Only 35% support immediate mandatory LCA. That rises to 59% when mandates come with subsidies or simplified tools. Norway's hard caps outperform the UK — but Denmark's hybrid model is the most directly transferable.

Recommendations

What needs to change

Each recommendation addresses a specific identified barrier — prioritised by severity, policy opportunity, and industry readiness.

01 — Remove the cost barrier
Free national LCA platform with UK-specific data
A government-funded tool eliminating software cost and the data-sourcing burden that consumes 68% of LCA time. Automated compliance reporting removes the need for specialist knowledge.
82% of respondents said this would drive adoption
02 — Make the business case real
Green Microgrants + tax relief on LCA-certified materials
Targeted financial instruments modelled on Denmark's subsidy architecture but extended to micro-enterprises. Addresses 79% of identified financial barriers and reframes LCA as an investment, not a cost.
91% agreed financial incentives would change behaviour
03 — Create a credible pathway
Phased mandate with Bronze-level certification entry point
Graduated compliance starting with public procurement, then residential above threshold, then private sector. Bronze certification cuts compliance costs by 50% — a realistic entry point for firms with no sustainability function.
Support rises from 35% to 59% when paired with practical assistance
